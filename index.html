On Minimalism

Posted on 2017-03-28

Being a minimalist does not necessarily mean possessing less, being acquainted with fewer friends, and meditating in an empty room now and then.

Minimalism is all about the space and time management of your brain. It is important to learn in today’s world how to refrain from information overload and inefficient multitasking. I could imagine some best-case scenarios. When I am writing an article in my room, I could feel that my room is empty though actually it’s not. When I need to check a particular term or concept online, Wikipedia to me is just an entry, not an encyclopedia. When I am deal with data on my laptop, it’s just a data processing machine and is incapable of doing anything else.

Minimalism is the habit of avoiding a state of richness in either material life or spiritual life but not deep.

极简是如何避免你的生活丰富而浅薄.

Christmas 2016

Posted on 2016-12-13

I walked by a monumental Christmas tree when the city was asleep. Neon lights kept all the shadows unseen and snowflakes were dancing like restless souls. It was time. It was time to embrace the cold and celebrate loneliness. The street looked dead, but never had I felt so alive.

I wrote this when I stepped across the snow-covered Queen’s Park at around 4 am, after a day and night’s lab work and paperwork. Some of the paperwork was just the aftermath of losing my passport in Stanford this summer. Two days before a friend from Mountain View had visited me in Toronto. She wanted to take a final glance at the great North American cities before she went back to China. She hated to use the phrase “settle down”. She is just like me, a fearless explorer, constantly trying to make discoveries about the world and more importantly, about herself. She stated that humans were born to be lonely and man should live for himself. I couldn’t say I agreed with her, but this doctrine did help when I was facing difficulties alone.

Anyway, I’m heading back home tomorrow. Though sadly I can’t stay at home for even half of the days. Here’s my schedule if anyone wants a quick meet and chat if there’s a chance.

人与城市

Posted on 2014-12-20

最近刚刚提交了加拿大PR的申请，忙完以后花了一个周末的时间开车走遍了多伦多的一些好久没去的角落。真正细细的品味以后，发现越来越爱这个城市，另一方面因为在申PR可能自然而然有一些心理上的变化吧。

今年暑假去美国中部看一个好友，和她聊起人与城市，说我大四的时候才发现自己爱上了南京，大学前面三年从来不会有的那种感觉。她说“应该是和你留恋这个城市里的某些人有关吧”。其实非也。虽然我确实很留恋在南京最后一年常和我在一块儿的那些人，但总体上来讲，对一个城市的爱，是随着对这个城市了解的加深而逐步加深的。大四的时候campus在市中心，自己也搬出了学校，住到了南京最典型的民国风格的小弄堂中——小巷名字我还特别喜欢，叫“苏州路”，能让我有家的感觉。那一年和父母、好友、来访问的教授和学者们一起走遍了南京大大小小的博物馆、历史街区和自然景点，对这个城市的历史、文化和民风了解地非常深入，于是越来越喜欢这个地方。在哪一天和谁走过哪里我现在还都记得清清楚楚，比如和ZJ一起去了甘熙故居，和ZXY一起走过了颐和路的民国使馆区，和1988年诺贝尔化学奖得主RH及其夫人去了秦淮河和栖霞山，和SW一起在玄武湖拍荷花然后夜闯紫峰的顶层和保安捉迷藏等等。当时我还有一个习惯，每天请一个人吃饭，每次都去一家口碑不错但没尝试过的新店，大半年下来把南京最好吃的都吃了个遍。

回忆就此打住。我只是举个例子，说说自己是怎么和一个城市慢慢建立起感情的。和我的家乡苏州就更不用说了，出生在城墙内就一辈子是苏州人。现在讲讲多伦多。
